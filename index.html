Axolotl (ak-suh-laa-tl)

Axolotls have the regenerative ability to fully regrow limbs.

  • Scientific name: Ambystoma mexicanum
  • Average Length: 6-18 inches
  • Average Lifespan: 10-15 years
  • Habitat: Xochimilco lake complex in Mexico City

Similar to Salamanders, Axolotls are amphibians. They live in water and come in a variety of colors including black, brown, gray, pale pink, and albino. They are carnivores and eat small fish and insects. They are a highly endangered species with a wild population of only about 700-1200 due to factors like overfishing, invasive species in their habitat, water pollution, and habitat loss.
